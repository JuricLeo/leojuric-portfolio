## I just started my YouTube channel
Why you might be asking? Well it's definetly not because I want to become an influencer. I don't really see myself as
one (at least for now).

## What for?
Instead, the main goal of the channel would be to **keep track of my progress in web development**. I'd also like to
**develop my speaking skills**, especially the english IT jargon. And in the end who knows, it might even **help someone**
out who is struggling with the same concepts that I'm talking about 🙂.

## What will I be posting?
In short: **web development content** (who would've guessed). Weather that be a tutorial, talking about a specific
topic - like hot trends, or deep diving into a framework. Whatever feels interesting or useful to me.
<br />
As for now, I have a few things in mind. I'm working on a personal project in `Laravel` + `React` which I plan on using
as a project for my **masters degree**. So I'll be talking about Laravel concepts that I'm using in that project.
<br />
For example:
- rate limiters
- policies
- migrations
- testing
- mailing
- and so on...

<br />
Even though the app is only for the Croatian market, I'm hoping to release the project to the public one day as well!

<br />
Along side that, I'm planning on releasing some content about `Astro` since I work with it a lot.

## Final thoughts
I'm excited to start this journey and I'm looking forward to sharing my knowledge, lets hope I get better at all the things
mentioned as well.

<br />
I'd also like to add a quick note: I got inspired to do this by [Chris Pennington](https://www.youtube.com/@CodinginPublic), also know
as **Coding in Public**. He's a great teacher and an even better developer (especially when it comes to Astro and DX).
If you want top tier content, make sure to check out his channel, you won't regret it.

<br />
And of course, you can check out my channel [here](https://www.youtube.com/@LeopoldJuric).
